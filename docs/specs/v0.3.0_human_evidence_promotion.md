# v0.3.0 human evidence promotion

This boundary is research-only. A grounded candidate becomes an in-memory
`EvidenceItem` only after a deterministic review packet, an explicit opaque
reviewer identifier, an explicit approve/reject/defer decision, and an explicit
field mapping have been checked. Recording an identifier does not establish any
credential, clinical qualification, scientific truth, experimental validation,
or clinical validity.

`HumanReviewPacket` contains source and content identities, extraction and audit
identities, and ordered candidate/card entries. It deliberately contains no full
source document or model reasoning. Pass cards still require a human decision.
Review cards require exactly one disposition for each warning rule; blocker cards
cannot be overridden. Decisions and packets are immutable and their identifiers
exclude operational timestamps and local paths.

An approval maps each existing `EvidenceItem` field explicitly (except identity,
the already-grounded quote, record hash, and review-chain provenance, which are
constructed from immutable inputs). Observation and interpretation are separate
mapped fields; neither candidate claim nor quote is silently repurposed as one.
The service rechecks source document identity, content hash, offsets, exact quote,
candidate/card/audit/packet/decision identities, and calls the existing evidence
validation contract. Audit warnings remain in manual-review provenance.

Promotion results retain the provenance chain from request/response through
candidate, card, audit, packet, and decision to the EvidenceItem. Rejection,
deferral, blocking, and validation failure construct no item. All results state
`persisted: false`: this module performs no retrieval, network/provider call,
subprocess, scoring, report rendering, or EvidenceStore/DuckDB/Parquet write.
Persistence, reviewer UI, signatures, and governance escalation are deferred.
